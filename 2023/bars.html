Arlington Rooftop Bar and GrillTo quote the great philosopher Reggie Jackson, is "the straw that stirs the drink." This festive occasion you have all come to know and love would not be possible had Josh and Jake moved in across the street from a coffee shop instead of a bar in late summer 2017.

Rooftop, in many ways, sparked the idea for JUNEFEST. Why have bar crawls indoors in DC when the weather is cold in December or March when you can make up your own holiday, walk across the street, get drunk outside, and wear Hawaiian shirts and bucket hats?

Cheers to Rooftop, the first bar in JUNEFEST as well as the first in our hearts! Oh... and you have to order an electric shark ü¶à

The PinemoorA recent addition to the JUNEFEST bar lineup, but many of you Arlington locals remember it in its past life as Clarendon Grill. And we are pleased to report that the ethos of Clarendon Grill lives on in Pinemoor. What we mean by that is Pinemoor is the type of place where you can show up and say without judgement "bring me two beers (or a full bottle of champagne or whatever your drink of choice) and then bring me two more every seven minutes" until the word 'seven' sounds like 'sthelfen.'

This is only further enabled by the fact that the good people at Pinemoor reserve the entire outdoor patio for us. So let's make sure that we're all on our best behavior, or at least leave enough gray area and tip well, so that they continue to have us back year in and year out.

Also, be sure to give a quick thought or pour two out for our fallen friend, Clarendon Grill.

Coco BWhat do Mickey Mantle, Kendall Roy and Coco B's all have in common? They all tried to replace titans in their respective fields. As you may know, Coco B's is the successor to the most iconic of all JUNEFEST bars, the late, great Whitlow's on Wilson. There's a lot of pressure on Coco B's to carry on Whitlow's legendary legacy. It's never easy to follow in the footsteps of an all time great. However, with the continued prime rooftop location and tiki bar theme, we are confident that this new, yet familiar, watering hole can live up to the hype. At the very least, let's hope we don't end up comparing it to Kendall Roy.

The LotThe nightcap and final stop of our JUNEFEST bar crawl. If the rest of your day is done correctly, the lights at The Lot (and just about everything else) might be a tad blurry. And that's ok so long as you are still upright and standing.

JUNEFEST is a marathon, not a sprint, except it's far more physically and mentally demanding than running 26.2 miles. So if you make it all the way to The Lot, you deserve to kick back, take in the blurry sights and sounds, and enjoy your accomplishment to the tune of an $8 White Claw. #LotBy9
